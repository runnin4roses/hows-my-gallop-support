# How's My Gallop — Support

How's My Gallop is a watchOS app that shows your gallop pace in meters per minute,
color-coded against USEA cross-country target speeds, and saves each ride as an
Equestrian Sports workout in Apple Health.

## Frequently asked questions

### The speed shows "--" or "Acquiring GPS" forever.
The app waits for a GPS fix accurate to within 20 meters before it will display a
speed. In tree cover or right after starting, this can take a minute. Move to open
sky if it persists. Make sure Location access for How's My Gallop is set to
"While Using" in Settings.

### Why m/min instead of mph or km/h?
Meters per minute is the standard unit USEA uses to define cross-country target
speeds (e.g. Novice = 400 m/min). The color zones in the app map directly to
those targets.

### Where do my rides go?
Each session is saved as an Equestrian Sports workout in the Apple Health app on
your paired iPhone. The GPS route is attached, so you can view it on the map in
the iPhone Fitness app.

### Does it work without my iPhone nearby?
Yes — How's My Gallop is a standalone watchOS app. As long as your Apple Watch has
GPS (Series 2 or later, or any Ultra), you can leave your phone at the trailer.

### The speed feels laggy / too jumpy.
The app smooths GPS speed readings so the number doesn't jitter every second. Top
speed is taken from the raw signal so genuine peaks aren't lost.

### My ride was cut short — did I lose it?
The workout is finalized when you tap Stop. If the app was force-quit mid-ride,
the partial workout may not be saved. Pausing and resuming is safe — it stays a
single workout.
